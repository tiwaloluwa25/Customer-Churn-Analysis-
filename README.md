# Customer-Churn-Analysis-
End-to-end customer churn analysis with Power BI dashboard and actionable business insights

📊 Project Overview

This project involves the creation of an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze and visualize customer attrition. The primary goal is to identify key drivers of churn, quantify revenue at risk, and provide actionable insights to improve customer retention strategies.

🚀 Key Insights

Based on the data visualization, three primary "churn triggers" were identified:

• Contract Vulnerability: Month-to-month subscribers exhibit a significantly higher churn rate compared to long-term contract holders.

• Payment Friction: Customers utilizing "Electronic Check" as a payment method are at the highest risk of leaving.

• The First Year Gap: Attrition is most aggressive within the first 12 months of customer tenure, highlighting a need for improved onboarding.

💰 Financial Impact

• Overall Churn Rate: 26.54%

• Total Revenue at Risk: $139.13K

• Success Metric: $6.5K in revenue has been successfully saved through current retention efforts.

🛠️ Tools Used

• Power BI: Data visualization and dashboard construction.

• DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Used for calculating measures like Churn Rate % and Revenue at Risk.

• Power Query: For data transformation and cleaning.

📂 Visualizations Included

1. Churn by Contract Type: A comparison of Month-to-Month vs. One/Two-year contracts.

2. Tenure Analysis: Breakdowns of how long customers stay before leaving.

3. Payment Method Impact: Correlation between billing methods and retention.

4. Demographic Filters: Slicers for Gender, Senior Citizen status, and Internet Service type for granular analysis.

💡 Recommendations

• Implement a migration campaign to move "Month-to-Month" users to "One-Year" contracts via targeted discounting.

• Develop a "New Customer Success" program specifically for users in the 0-12 month tenure bracket.

• Incentivize the adoption of automated payment methods (Credit Card/Bank Transfer) to reduce payment-related friction.
